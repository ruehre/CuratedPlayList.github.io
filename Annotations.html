Annotations

Analysis on the songs chosen and how they illustrate resistance.

1. “Snow on the Bluff” - J Cole

Name: “Snow on the Bluff” - J Cole
Year: 2020
Genre: Rap
This is J Cole's newest song in response to the 2020 BLM movement. It talks about how people look up to him and how he along with other celebrities need to step up and be the voice that people need. J Cole is hands down top 3 of the best rappers of this era. He is lyrically a genius so for him to put out this song it just goes to show that he cares and that people will listen and respond to it.

2. “Otherside of America” - Meek Mill

Name: “Otherside of America” - Meek Mill
Year: 2020
Genre: Rap
In “Otherside of America”, Meek Mill powerfully shares his side of the story. The song is started off with a speech by the current President highlighting the difficulties that plague Black societies. Meek Mill then goes into a song, rapping about the community he comes from and the lengths he had to go to just to get by. His song highlights the struggles of certain Black communities and asks people to think before they judge those people’s actions. The song ends with Mill asking a reporter if he grew up in those conditions, wouldn’t he carry a gun too. The reporter responds, “yeah, I probably would”.

3. “The Bigger Picture” - Lil Baby

Name: “The Bigger Picture” - Lil Baby
Year: 2020
Genre: Rap
This song was released in response to the death of George Floyd and the protests and riots that ensued. Lil Baby uses this song to highlight police brutality and the condition of Black people in America today. He desperately calls for change and change now, so that nobody has to grow up in the lifestyle that he was forced to live. His song asks for people to see “the bigger picture” and work together to resist against a corrupt system and create a better future for everyone.

This song was written to acknowledge the pain people are facing and spread the message of the BLM movement.

6. “Stand Up” - Cynthia Erivo

Name: “Stand Up” - Cynthia Erivo
Year: 2019
Genre: Gospel
Produced for the 2019 film “Harriet”, which depicts the life story of Harriet Tubman and her role in helping slaves escape to freedom through the Underground Railroad, this song highlights the strength it took to rebel against the terrible institution of slavery. Resistance is seen in the lyrics, calling for people to stand up and fight against oppression in order to create a better life for each other despite the costs. It includes some musical Africanisms, such as humming and clapping, which foster a sense of community and the power and freedom that comes with that.

7. “Brown Skin Girl” - Blue Ivy, Beyonce, Saint Jhn, Wizkid

Name: “Brown Skin Girl” - Blue Ivy, Beyonce, Saint Jhn, Wizkid
Year: 2019
Genre: R&B
As Black women are often one of the most excluded groups in society, this song shares an important message of self-acceptance. European beauty ideals are often dominant in the media, occasionally making others who do not fit that mold feel as if they do not qualify as beautiful. This song challenges those thoughts, calling for Black girls to recognize and embrace the beauty and power in themselves despite whatever the world may throw at them.

8. “Melanin” - Ciara ft. Lupita Nyongo’o, Ester Dean, City Girls, La La

Name: “Melanin” - Ciara ft. Lupita Nyongo’o, Ester Dean, City Girls, La La
Year: 2019
Genre: Hip Hop / Rap
Resisting against European standards of beauty, this song illustrates Black women taking charge of themselves and their sexuality. Through upbeat music, highlighting the word melanin, and comparing it to sweet things like chocolate, this song becomes an anthem for Black women to be proud of their skin color and own it. This song resists against a history of darker skin being seen as “ugly” or “undesirable” by recognizing it as beautiful and powerful. The inclusion of many artists in the creation of this song creates a sense of community, while also highlighting the fact that all are different and beautiful.

10. “This is America”- Childish Gambino

Name: “This is America”- Childish Gambino
Year: 2018
Genre: Hip Hop / Rap
Released during a time of political and social unrest, this song depicts the difficulty of Black life in America. With the juxtaposition of happy, upbeat choruses and the dark, aggressive verses, we get a glimpse of the harsh reality that may face. The music video is particularly important, with imagery alluding to that of Jim Crow, religious affect, and police violence. It was chosen not only due to its important and unique sound and imagery but also due to its popularity and significance to our current state in America.

13. “Be Free” - J. Cole

Name: “Be Free” - J. Cole
Year: 2017
Genre: Blues / Hip Hop
Calling attention to the ongoing discrimination and oppression of Black individuals, this song is a powerful anthem for freedom. In his lyrics “All we wanna do is take these chains off | All we wanna do is break the chains of pain | All we wanna do is be free”, J. Cole clearly highlights the fact that the effects of slavery and Jim Crow segregation are still haunting many and making it difficult to live life freely and safely. Sometimes, for White and non-Black people of color it can be easy to fall into the trap of thinking so much progress has been made, that things are better now. Through his lyrics and music video, J. Cole’s song strongly resists that idea and calls for change to continue so they can finally “be free”.

37. “Free Man” - South Shore Commission

Name: “Free Man” - South Shore Commission
Year: 1975
Genre: R&B / Soul
This song speaks to a man’s desire to be free to express himself and his sexuality. Author Mark Anthony Neal writes that the song ‘Free Man’ was “acknowledged as an anthem of Black male homosexuality in the 1970s”. During this time period, many artists began including topics of sexuality and homosexuality in their work as well as experimenting with new forms of self-expression.

38. “Lady Marmalade”- Labelle

Name: “Lady Marmalade”- Labelle
Year: 1974
Genre: Pop
This song, as stated by writer Mark Anthony Neal was used to resist sexual repression, specifically African American sexual repression, during the post-civil rights time period. By using this song to express sexual freedom, Labelle uses music as a means to resist sexual repression. This song is significant because, as explained by Mark Anthony Neal, it was written during a time when sexual freedom was used as an excuse to condone acts of sexual violence towards African American women through the spreading and entertainment of the myth that African American women were “insatiable”.

43. “Go Down Moses” - Louis Armstrong

Name: “Go Down Moses” - Louis Armstrong
Year: 1958
Genre: Spiritual
This song was a popular spiritual. African spirituals were commonly known to have double meanings and be used as emotional outlets and tools to communicate. By using music to communicate with each other, enslaved people were able to subvert and resist the people and institutions trying to control them. ‘Go Down Moses’ is especially significant as it was used to help slaves escape to freedom, specifically by Harriet Tubman.

44. “We Shall Overcome” by Charles Albert Tindley

Name: “We Shall Overcome” by Charles Albert Tindley
Year: 1900
Genre: Folk
“We Shall Overcome” is a commonly known song associated with the civil rights movement. Through its simple lyric “we shall overcome”, this song spreads hope into every heart. The short phrase and repetition are common Africanisms used to foster audience participation. This song is capable of being sung by many, especially in a large group during a protest or march. Singing together creates a sense of community and good feeling that is part of what makes this simple song so powerful. This song is one that fills one up and makes them feel as if they can achieve anything.
